02 · User Research

Home Automation Adoption Study In Progress

Qualitative research exploring how households think about delegating tasks to robots — trust barriers, privacy concerns, and adoption drivers. Directly relevant to designing beta programs for home robotics.

Methodology

Semi-structured interviews with 5–8 participants across different household types. Research plan and interview guide managed in Notion. Analysis uses affinity mapping and thematic coding.

n = 5–8 Semi-structured Affinity Mapping Notion
🔍

Research Questions

What tasks would households delegate to a robot? What are the trust and privacy thresholds? How does the presence of children or pets change expectations? What does "safety" mean to non-technical users?

📋

Deliverables

User personas, trust spectrum framework, safety requirements matrix, and product recommendations for beta program onboarding — including suggested A/B tests for expectation-setting during deployment.

⚠ Safety-Critical Lens

This research treats safety as a first-class product concern, not an afterthought. Interview protocol includes dedicated modules on physical safety expectations (especially with children and pets), data privacy mental models, and incident reporting preferences. Findings will map directly to safety scorecards and incident tracking frameworks relevant to programs like Sunday's Founding Family Beta.

🧪 A/B Testing Opportunities

The study identifies concrete A/B testing hypotheses for home robotics deployment: onboarding flow variants (guided tour vs. self-exploration), privacy consent framing (opt-in vs. opt-out defaults), feedback collection cadence (daily check-in vs. weekly digest), and incident reporting UX (in-app vs. proactive robot-initiated). Each hypothesis is documented with expected impact, metrics, and minimum sample size estimates.
